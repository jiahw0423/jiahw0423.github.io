Welcome to my personal website!

I am Haowen Jia, a fourth-year undergraduate student in the School of Physics and Astronomy at Sun Yat-sen University, majoring in Physics.

I am currently working with Prof. Maijia Liao at Northeastern University. Previously, I worked as a research assistant under Prof. Jie Zhuang at the Suzhou Institute of Biomedical Engineering and Technology, Chinese Academy of Sciences. At Sun Yat-sen University, I am advised by Prof. Shuangqiang Liu and Prof. Zhu Li.

My research interests lie at the interface of physics and biology. I am particularly interested in understanding how physical principles govern neuronal structures and dynamics, with a current focus on microtubule stability and geometric confinement effects using FRAP-based quantitative approaches.

Outside of research, I enjoy playing the saxophone and Chinese bamboo flute, playing basketball and football, and capturing moments through photography.
